“Stairs are biased towards people who can walk, books are biased towards people who can see and even most programming languages are biased towards English speaking people... This is to say that technologies are not neutral, they are embedded with the biases of the folks who produced them.“

- Quote from Nick Britz’ website, “Net Art Rocks”

An interactive and artistic exploration into the ideas surrounding digital literacy and net neutrality. Technology - whether small (such as a file extension) or big (such as the internet) - is never neutral, they are biased according to the person/people who produced them.

“I'd like to think that computers are neutral, a tool like any other... But there is something in the system itself, in the formal logic of programs and data, that recreates the world in its own image. [...]We think we are creating the system, but the system is also creating us. We build the system, we live in its midst, and we are changed.”

- Excerpt from Close To The Machine, by Ellen Ullman

The project concept is to create a visual representation of some of the most well recognised and used platforms on the internet, which, as the user interacts with them begin to reveal the complexities of each.

The complexities presented reveal the ‘hidden’ information about these online platforms: whether this in the form of representing who the creators are, where they are from (Eurocentric or Amerocentric biases?), their gender and race; what political stance the platform or creators hold; what the platform was intended for; what the platform tracks or knows about you; and possibly any contradictory or controversial information about said platform.

This begins to paint a complex world, one which questions - and asks the viewer to question - what the inherent biases of the internet are, what the agenda may be and whether or not this is harmful. The intention is to increase the audience’s sense of awareness on these issues, and thus, heighten digital literacy in terms of net neutrality.

As the parts swing, rotate and move the viewer is both in and out of control - physically experiencing the effects of the bias of me - the creator - as well as visually exploring the biases of the platforms presented.

Basically, the project is representing net neutrality and the inherent bias of all created platforms that we use online, by exploding the information relating to very popular user platforms onto a 3D map that the user needs to try to engage with.
